SQL Normalization

Organizing tables to reduce redundancy and improve integrity

What is Normalization?

Normalization is the process of organizing database tables and their relationships. It reduces data redundancy and ensures data integrity.

Benefits of Normalization

  • Eliminates duplicate data
  • Reduces data anomalies
  • Improves database consistency
  • Optimizes storage

1. First Normal Form (1NF)

Ensures that each column contains atomic (indivisible) values and each row is unique.

-- Example table not in 1NF Student_Courses +----+---------+------------------+ | ID | Name | Courses | +----+---------+------------------+ | 1 | Alice | Math, Science | | 2 | Bob | English | +----+---------+------------------+ -- After 1NF +----+---------+--------+ | ID | Name | Course | +----+---------+--------+ | 1 | Alice | Math | | 1 | Alice | Science| | 2 | Bob | English| +----+---------+--------+

2. Second Normal Form (2NF)

Achieved when the table is in 1NF and all non-key columns are fully dependent on the primary key.

-- Remove partial dependency Student_Courses +----+---------+--------+ Instructor | +----+---------+--------+------------+ | 1 | Alice | Math | Mr. A | | 1 | Alice | Science| Mr. B | -- Split into two tables Students +----+---------+ | ID | Name | +----+---------+ Courses +--------+------------+ | Course | Instructor | +--------+------------+

3. Third Normal Form (3NF)

Achieved when the table is in 2NF and all non-key columns are not transitively dependent on the primary key.

-- Avoid transitive dependency Employees +----+--------+ DeptName | DeptLocation | +----+--------+----------+--------------+ -- Split into Employees +----+--------+ DeptID | +----+--------+--------+ Departments +--------+----------+--------------+ | DeptID | DeptName | DeptLocation | +--------+----------+--------------+

Conclusion

Normalization organizes data to minimize redundancy and maintain integrity. Most databases are normalized up to 3NF for efficiency and clarity.
